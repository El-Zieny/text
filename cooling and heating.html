Cooling & Heating

BASICS

How Car Heating and Ventilation Systems Work

Modern cars always allow fresh air to enter the cabin, even when the windows are closed. Air enters through vents at the front of the car and is pushed inside by a fan. This air can stay cool, or it can be warmed using heat taken from the engine. The engine produces a lot of heat as it runs, and instead of wasting that heat, the car uses it to warm the cabin. The warm coolant from the engine passes through a small radiator called the heater core. As air flows through it, the air becomes warm and enters the cabin. This system keeps windows clear from fog, maintains a comfortable temperature, and ensures there is always fresh air circulating.
